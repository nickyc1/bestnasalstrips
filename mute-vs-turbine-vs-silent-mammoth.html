Mute vs Turbine vs Silent Mammoth

The three most-recommended internal nasal dilators, side by side. All three sit inside the nostrils and open the nasal valve directly, but they're built for very different jobs. Here's which one fits you.

Our team Updated June 16, 2026 ~6 min read
The short answer

Mute is the budget entry point, adjustable, three sizes, reusable, ~$20-30, the cheapest way to test internal dilators. Turbine is for athletes and daytime, firmer material that stays seated during activity. Silent Mammoth is the most advanced, under 0.5mm profile, customizable stainless frame, tip-anchored, ~60-day lifespan, built for 24/7 wear at a premium price. All three open the valve more directly than any external strip.

Which should you buy?

Buy Mute if you're new to internal dilators and want the cheapest, lowest-risk way to find out whether one helps your nighttime breathing. Three sizes and adjustable expansion make it easy to dial in, and the ~$20-30 price means you're not out much if the in-nose feel isn't for you. The trade-off: it's reusable for only about ten nights per unit, and some users find it less expansive than premium models.

Buy Turbine if your need is daytime, running, cycling, training, or any activity where you want more nasal airflow and a dilator that won't work loose when you move. Its firmer material is the point. It's not the one to reach for if your only goal is quiet, comfortable sleep.

Buy Silent Mammoth if you want the most capable internal dilator and plan to wear it a lot, including during the day. The tip anchor and adjustable stainless frame hold it in place better than a loose in-nose plug, the under-0.5mm profile keeps it near-invisible, and the ~60-day lifespan softens the premium price over time. It's the standout pick for nasal valve collapse.

Reality check: even well-fitted internal dilators can work loose or cause minor irritation overnight, the most common complaint in this whole category. Sizing is everything. Silent Mammoth's tip anchor is the most resistant to falling out; a poorly sized Mute is the most likely to.
Assessment reflects our hands-on testing of all three combined with manufacturer specs and patterns across aggregated verified buyer reviews as of June 2026. None of them treats obstructive sleep apnea. If you gasp, choke, or stop breathing during sleep, see a doctor.

FAQ

Which is the best internal nasal dilator overall?
Silent Mammoth is the most advanced, <0.5mm profile, adjustable stainless frame, tip-anchored, built for 24/7 wear with a ~60-day lifespan. Mute is the better budget entry; Turbine is the firmer pick for athletes and daytime use.
What is the difference between Mute and Turbine?
Both are Rhinomed internal dilators. Mute is softer, adjustable, and sleep-focused, reusable for about ten nights per unit. Turbine uses a firmer material aimed at athletes and daytime exercise, so it stays put during activity. Mute prioritizes comfort and price; Turbine prioritizes hold during movement.
Is Silent Mammoth worth the premium price?
If you want a dilator you can wear around the clock, yes, the tip anchor, adjustable frame, near-invisible profile, and 24/7 positioning justify it for nasal valve collapse or all-day needs. If you only need help at night and want to test the category cheaply, Mute is the smarter first buy.
Which internal dilator is best for athletes?
Turbine. Its firmer material is designed for daytime exercise and stays seated during activity. Mute is softer and geared toward sleep; Silent Mammoth is built for continuous 24/7 wear rather than high-intensity movement specifically.
How long do these internal dilators last?
Mute is reusable for roughly ten nights per unit. Turbine is reusable and designed for repeated daytime sessions. Silent Mammoth has the longest stated lifespan at around 60 days, reflecting its medical-grade silicone over a stainless frame and premium positioning.
