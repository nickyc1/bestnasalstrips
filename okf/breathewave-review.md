# Breathewave Review (2026)

Hands-on, bought at retail, not sponsored. Score 6.0/10.

Breathewave is an internal (in-nose) nasal dilator marketed as "the world's most comfortable nasal dilator." It inserts into the nostrils and expands to hold the passages open from the inside, with no magnets and no adhesive. The $50 starter pack includes three sizes (small, medium, large) plus three carrying cases and is fully reusable.

## Scores (out of 10)
- Unboxing: 8.0, premium; three sizes included
- Setup: 6.5, pick a size and insert; no skin prep or adhesive
- Comfort & ease: 5.0, the in-nose feel was uncomfortable all night for us
- Pricing & value: 5.0, priciest starter tested, but no consumables
- Customer support: 7.0, DTC; shipping and support matter
- Effectiveness: 6.0, opens the nose with nothing on the face, but comfort limited wear

## Verdict
The concept is the best of the bunch: no magnets, no adhesive, just insert and breathe, with three sizes to dial in fit. But internal dilators sit inside the nostrils, and we found Breathewave uncomfortable to wear through the night, the opposite of its "most comfortable" claim. It opens the nose, but comfort determines whether you keep it in, and that was the dealbreaker. Best for people who tolerate internal dilators well.
