# Intake vs Breathe Right (2026)

Buy **Intake** if you snore most nights and want the strongest, most reliable opening, it pulls the nostrils wider, stays put when you move or sweat, and peels off painlessly because there is no adhesive. Buy **Breathe Right** if you only need help occasionally or want the cheapest way to try a nasal aid. Intake costs more upfront but is reusable; Breathe Right is cheaper per box but single-use.

## Scores

- Intake Breathing: 92 / 100 (our pick for nightly use)
- Breathe Right Extra Strength: 85 / 100 (best for occasional use)

## Comparison

| What matters | Intake Breathing | Breathe Right Extra |
|--------------|------------------|---------------------|
| Type | External reusable dilator (clips in nostril rim) | Single-use adhesive strip on nose bridge |
| Airflow opening | Stronger, more direct | Moderate, lifts skin only |
| Stays on (movement/sweat) | Yes, no adhesive dependence | Inconsistent, can lift with sweat |
| Comfort & removal | No adhesive pull | Can tug or redden sensitive skin |
| Hygiene | Reusable, needs cleaning | Fresh strip every night |
| Upfront cost | Higher | Low |
| Cost per night (heavy use) | Lower (reusable) | Adds up |
| Travel | Good, bring a case | Grab and go |
| Best for | Nightly snorers, athletes, side sleepers | Occasional use, first-timers, budget |

## Recommendation

Choose Intake for nightly snoring, sweat or movement, sensitive skin, or maximum airflow. Choose Breathe Right for occasional colds/allergies, first-time trials, or lowest upfront cost. A common path: start with Breathe Right to confirm your snoring is nose-driven, then graduate to Intake for nightly use.

Neither product treats obstructive sleep apnea.
