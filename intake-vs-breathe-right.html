Intake vs Breathe Right

The reusable dilator that everyone's switching to versus the drugstore strip that defined the category. We scored both on the four things that matter overnight.

The short answer

Buy Intake if you snore most nights and want the strongest, most reliable opening, it pulls the nostrils wider, stays put when you move or sweat, and peels off painlessly because there's no adhesive. Buy Breathe Right if you only need help occasionally or want the cheapest way to try a nasal aid tonight. Intake costs more upfront but is reusable; Breathe Right is cheaper per box but single-use.

Which should you buy?

Choose Intake if you snore most nights, sweat or move in your sleep, have sensitive skin that the adhesive irritates, or want the maximum nasal opening you can get without putting something deep inside your nose. The stronger, steadier airflow and painless removal are the difference most switchers notice first.

Choose Breathe Right if you only need help during a cold or allergy flare, you're trying a nasal aid for the first time and don't want to commit, or you value grabbing a fresh, no-cleaning strip and tossing it in the morning. It's the cheapest way to find out whether a nasal aid helps your snoring at all.

A common path: start with a box of Breathe Right to confirm your snoring is nose-driven, then graduate to Intake once you know it works and you're using it nightly.

FAQ

Is Intake better than Breathe Right?
For nightly use, yes for most people, it opens the nostrils more, stays put when you move or sweat, and removes painlessly. Breathe Right wins on upfront cost and grab-and-go convenience for occasional use.
Does Intake or Breathe Right open your nose more?
Intake. As an external dilator it pulls the nostrils outward from inside the rim, a stronger and more direct opening than an adhesive strip that lifts only the skin over the nasal valve.
Is Intake cheaper over time?
For heavy nightly users, usually yes, each Intake band is reusable for about a week, so cost per night drops below single-use strips. Occasional users still pay less with a cheap box of Breathe Right.
Can I use Intake or Breathe Right with a CPAP?
Nasal aids can make nasal-pillow CPAP more comfortable by easing nasal airflow, but they don't replace CPAP and aren't a treatment for sleep apnea. Check with your sleep clinician.
